Customer Journey Simulation Blueprint

An interactive exercise to refine our customer engagement process.

Setup: The Hypothetical Customer

Company Name & Industry: Innovate Corp, a mid-size SaaS company

Size & Maturity: 500 employees, technically proficient but resource-constrained

Core Business Problem: Needs to automate their manual customer onboarding data entry process.

Key Stakeholders: Head of Operations, a technical lead, a project manager

Phase 1: Project Kick-off & Discovery

Goal: Align all internal and customer stakeholders on the project plan, timeline, and detailed requirements, ensuring a shared understanding of scope and a solid foundation for the project.

Owner & Contributors

Simulation Action Items

  • Assign roles and role-play the internal and customer kick-off calls.
  • Draft a high-level project plan on a whiteboard or document.
  • Collaboratively list the technical questions you would ask the "customer".
  • Discuss and define the communication plan and meeting cadence.

Phase 1: Notes & Feedback

Phase 1: Scoring

Phase 2: Implementation & Build

Goal: Execute the project plan efficiently, manage change effectively, and maintain clear, proactive communication to keep the project on track and stakeholders informed.

Owner & Contributors

Simulation Action Items

  • Facilitator: Present a "small" out-of-scope change request from the customer.
  • Team: Discuss and role-play the response for the change request.
  • Facilitator: Introduce a major technical blocker (e.g., customer API isn't working).
  • Team: Walk through the internal and external escalation path for the blocker.

Phase 2: Notes & Feedback

Phase 2: Scoring

Phase 3: UAT, Go-Live & Handoff

Goal: Ensure the solution is thoroughly validated by the customer and deployed smoothly into production, followed by a clean, comprehensive transition to the Customer Success and Support teams for long-term customer success.

Owner & Contributors

Simulation Action Items

  • Review a mock list of UAT feedback (bugs vs. enhancements).
  • Role-play the conversation to prioritize feedback for go-live.
  • Walk through the go-live deployment checklist step-by-step.
  • Draft the formal handoff email and documentation for CS & Support.

Phase 3: Scoring

4. Overall Debrief & Action Items

Discussion Prompts

  • What were the biggest friction points or recurring issues we identified across all phases?
  • Where did our inter-departmental communication and handoffs break down or excel?
  • What is the single most impactful thing we can change in our process to improve the customer experience?
